Dizzy Gillespie

Dizzy playing his signature bent trumpet Source: Wikipedia

John Birks "Dizzy" Gillespie was an American Jazz trumpeter, bandleader, composer, educator, and singer

Life and Career

  • 1917 - Born in Cheraw, South Carolina
  • 1935 - Attended Laurinburg Institute in North Carolina where he earned a music scholarship.
  • 1935 - First professional job with the Frank Fairfax Orchestra
  • 1937 - Joined the Teddy Hill Orchestra as second trumpet. It was here that he made his first recording, "King Porter Stomp"
  • 1939 - Left Teddy Hill's orchestra to join Cab Calloway and his orchestra
  • 1940 - Married Lorraine Willis on May 9th.
  • 1940 - Recorded "Pickin' the Cabbage", one of his earliest compositions, with Cab Calloway
  • 1941 - Calloway fired Gillespie over an altercation between the two. Which involved someone spitballs thrown in rehearsal... ultimately ending with Gillespie stabbing Calloway in the leg and getting fired after the altercation.
  • 1943 - Dizzy did not serve in WWII and was labeled a "4-F"; not suited for service. At the time he told the local board: "in this stage of my life here in the United States whose foot has been in my ass?" and "So if you put me out there with a gun in my hand and tell me to shoot at the enemy, I'm liable to create a case of 'mistaken identity' of who I might shoot."
  • 1942-1947 - The rise of bebop; Dizzy would be seen playing with the likes of John Coltrane, Lalo Schifrin, andYusef Lateef to name a few.
  • During this time he composed some of his most popular compositions such as Groovin' High, Woody 'n' You, and Salt Peanuts, to name a few. These compositions were radically different harmonically and rhythmically from the swing music that was popular at the time, and helped define bebop's unique style.
  • 1953 - While throwing a party for his wife Lorraine in Manhattan, his trumpet suffered an accident and the bell was bent up. He loved the sound that the damaged trumpet made so much that he had a special trumpet made with the bell deliberately bent up 45 degrees, giving Dizzy his signature sound and look.
  • 1956 - Organized a band to tour the Middle East. This tour was so well received world wide that he was dubbed 'The Ambassador of Jazz'
  • 1980s - Dizzy Gillespie led the United Nation Orchestra and starred in The Winter Lisbon a crime drama about a jazz pianist who falls for a dangerous woman while abroad in Portugal, which he also performed some of the music for.
  • 1991 - Suffered a crisis which turned out to be pancreatic cancer, he performed one more night of his 1991 engagement at Kimball's East in Emeryville California, and canceled the rest of the tour.
  • 1993 - Died in Englewood, New Jersey from pancreatic cancer and was buried in Flushing Cemetery, Queens, New York City.
